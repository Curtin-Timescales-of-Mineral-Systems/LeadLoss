                           211111.1: meta-andesite, McCrae Bore
          (Madoonga Formation; Southern Cross Supergroup, Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                                SS Romano, IOH Fielding, MTD Wingate, Y Lu and TJ Ivanic


Location and sampling
BELELE SG50-11, KALLI 2344                                                        Zone 50, MGA 537838E 7013837N
Warox Site TJIMKT000131                                                               Sampled on 8 September 2014
This sample was collected from a low-lying outcrop on the north side of Weld Range, about 10.3 km south-southwest of
Mardoonganna Hills, 7.8 km northeast of Errida Spring and 3.5 km west-northwest of McCrae Bore.

Geological context
The unit sampled is a meta-andesite with lenticular compositional heterogeneity assigned to the Madoonga Formation of
the 3010–2920 Ma Southern Cross Supergroup (Ivanic et al., 2022). The Southern Cross Supergroup consists of
metamorphosed basalt, andesite–dacite–rhyolite, and variable proportions of sedimentary rocks exposed in several belts
along a northwest-trending, 750 km-long corridor from Ravensthorpe in the south to the Weld Range in the north within
the Youanmi Terrane (Ivanic et al., 2022). This supergroup contains six formations, i.e., the Wongan, Madoonga, Gossan
Hill, Mount Gibson, Honman, and Annabelle Volcanics Formations, which are unconformably overlain by the c. 2.8 Ga
Norie and Polelle Groups of the Murchison Supergroup (Van Kranendonk et al., 2013; Ivanic et al., 2022). The 2979–2969
Ma Madoonga Formation includes metamorphosed rhyolitic volcanic and volcaniclastic rocks with lesser psammite and
pelite and minor komatiitic basalt (Wyche et al., 2014). A sample of metarhyolite of the Madoonga Formation located
about 9.4 km to the east yielded an igneous crystallization age of 2977 ± 3 Ma (GSWA 155569, Wingate et al., 2019). A
metasandstone located about 13.9 km to the east indicates a maximum depositional age of 2969 ± 3 Ma (GSWA 184112,
Wingate et al., 2008).

Petrographic description
The sample is a recrystallized amphibolite characterized by a lenticular compositional heterogeneity (Fig. 1), consisting of
about 30–35% hornblende, 27–30% sericitised plagioclase, 20–25% aggregates of fine-grained quartz and unaltered
plagioclase, 8–10% microcline, 7–10% altered pyroxene and accessory zircon and titanite. Hornblende is euhedral to
subhedral, <0.05 mm in diameter, and form poorly-defined bands and lenses of non-aligned grains. The hornblende-rich
                                                              1
zones also contain fine-grained sericitised minerals (most likely completely altered plagioclase), and lesser amount of fine-
grained quartz. Former pyroxene, which occurs as elongate grains up to 0.5 mm long, is completely replaced by epidote
and fine-grained brown iron oxides. Hornblende-poor lenses comprise recrystallized aggregates of quartz, plagioclase and
K-feldspar >0.1 mm in size, and anhedral and micropoikilitic grains of microcline and rare plagioclase <2.5 mm in
length. The rock has been completely recrystallized to a granophyric and partly compositionally segregated texture at
amphibolite facies. The presence of metamorphic amphibole in this rock is interpreted to be of secondary importance to the
protolith being intermediate in composition, as indicated by the presence of 20% primary magmatic quartz.

Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are colourless and mainly subhedral. The crystals are up to 150 µm long, and equant to
elongate with aspect ratios up to 5:1. In cathodoluminescence (CL) images, most zircons exhibit concentric zoning. A CL
image of representative zircons is shown in Figure 2.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 21–22 October 2021, using SHRIMP-B. Fifteen analyses of the M257 standard were obtained
during the session, of which 11 analyses indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 0.80% (1σ) and a
238U/206Pb* calibration uncertainty of 0.32% (1σ). Calibration uncertainties are included in the errors of 238U/206Pb*
ratios and dates listed in Table 1 (provided in the Links section). Common-Pb corrections were applied to all analyses using
contemporaneous isotopic compositions determined according to the model of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
A total of 15 analyses were obtained from 15 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1 and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig.
3).

Interpretation
The analyses are concordant to slightly discordant (Fig. 3), and form a single group, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb* ratios.

Group I comprises 15 analyses (Table 1), which yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 2977 ± 5 Ma (MSWD =
0.87).
The date of 2977 ± 5 Ma for the 15 analyses in Group I is interpreted as the igneous crystallization age of the meta-
andesite.

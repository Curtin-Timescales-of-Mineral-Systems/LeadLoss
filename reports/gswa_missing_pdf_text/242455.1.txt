                 242455.1: metagranodiorite, Mardoonganna Hills
             (Mindoolah Tonalite, Thundelarra Supersuite; Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                                      SS Romano, Y Lu, IOH Fielding and TJ Ivanic


Location and sampling
BELELE SG50-11, KALLI 2344                                                     Zone 50, MGA 541661E 7018077N
Warox Site TJIKAL190040                                                            Sampled on 1 September 2019
This sample was collected from an outcrop about 7.8 km west-southwest of Mindodah Well, 5.9 km east-southeast of
Sahara Well, and 5.2 km south of Mardoonganna Hills.

Geological context
The unit sampled is a metagranodiorite (Fig. 1) of the Mindoolah Tonalite of the Thundelarra Supersuite (Ivanic et al.,
2022). The Mindoolah Tonalite intruded the 2979–2969 Ma felsic volcanics-dominated Madoonga Formation in the Weld
Range area (Ivanic et al., 2022). A metarhyolite of the Madoonga Formation, collected about 5.8 km to the southeast,
yielded an igneous crystallization age of 2977 ± 3 Ma (GSWA 155569, Wingate et al. 2019). A metarhyolite clast derived
from the Madoonga Formation, collected about 10.6 km to the east-southeast, yielded an igneous crystallization age of
2979 ± 3 Ma (GSWA 193972, Wingate et al. 2012). A metasandstone of the Madoonga Formation, collected about 10.1
km to the east-southeast, yielded a single age population with maximum depositional age of 2969 ± 3 Ma (GSWA 184112,
Wingate et al. 2008). Together with the geochronological data from the Madoonga Formation, the results from the current
sample imply that a large area of c. 2.97 Ga crust is exposed in the Weld Range area.

Petrographic description
The sample is a fine-grained, weakly porphyritic, and spherulitic granophyric metagranodiorite, consisting of about 40–45%
plagioclase, 40% quartz, 10–15% K-feldspar, 4–5% biotite/chlorite, and accessory muscovite, apatite and zircon.
Plagioclase occurs as sericitized, subhedral, and tabular grains that are mostly <1 mm in size with some less sericitized
grains forming the finer quartz–feldspar groundmass. Quartz occurs as anhedral to subhedral grains up to 2 mm in size, a
few with lobate grain boundaries. Smaller quartz grains are abundant in the quartz–feldspar groundmass, and in the
granophyric patches. K-feldspar mainly occurs in the round, granophyric lenses, which range up to 2.5 mm in diameter.
Brown biotite is anhedral to subhedral, forming laths up to 0.5 mm in size. Smaller biotite grains (<0.1 mm) occur
                                                            1
throughout the quartz–feldspar groundmass. Anhedral muscovite up to 0.2 mm is rare. Apatite mainly occurs as euhedral
grains to 0.05 mm in length with a few grains up to 0.2 mm long. The texture of this rock is consistent with it being a high-
level intrusive rock. It lacks a tectonic fabric but appears to have undergone some quartz recrystallization at greenschist
facies conditions.

Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are colourless to light brown and mainly subhedral to euhedral. The crystals are up to 300
µm long, with aspect ratios up to 4:1. In cathodoluminescence (CL) images, concentric zoning is ubiquitous, and many
grains contain inclusions. A CL image of representative zircons is shown in Figure 2.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 23–25 January 2023, using SHRIMP-B. Seventeen analyses of the M257 standard were
obtained during the session, of which 14 analyses indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 0.59%
(1σ) and a 238U/206Pb* calibration uncertainty of 0.24% (1σ). Calibration uncertainties are included in the errors of
238U/206Pb* ratios and dates listed in Table 1 (provided in the Links section). Common-Pb corrections were applied to all
analyses using contemporaneous isotopic compositions determined according to the model of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
Fifteen analyses were obtained from 14 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1 and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig. 3).

Interpretation
The analyses are concordant to moderately discordant (Fig. 3), and form a single group, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb*
ratios.

Group I comprises 15 analyses (Table 1), which yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 2973 ± 4 Ma (MSWD =
1.2).

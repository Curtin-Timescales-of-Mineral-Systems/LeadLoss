         142832: metamorphosed intermediate rock, Whundo

Location and sampling
YARRALOOLA (SF 50–6)                                                    116°55'23"E 21°06'42"S
Sampled on 23 September 1996
The sample was taken from the north side of a ridge leading up to a prominent hill topped with a
cairn, about 700 m from the base of the Whundo Group and about 3.6 km south of Whundo.

Tectonic unit/relations
This sample is from a 5–10 m thick, metamorphosed, felsic to intermediate unit within a
metabasaltic sequence located less than 700 m from the base of the Nallana Formation of the
Whundo Group. The sequence has a bedding-parallel foliation.

Petrographic description
The principal minerals present in this sample are sericite, quartz and chlorite, with accessory
opaques, leucoxene, biotite, apatite, and epidote. This rock is completely altered to sericite,
chlorite and quartz and this has obscured much of the original texture. Rectangular masses of
sericite embedded in generally fine-grained quartz mosaic, sericite, and fine foliated clots of
chlorite–leucoxene, suggest the rock probably contained abundant plagioclase laths, interstitial
quartz, plagioclase, and mafic minerals. The presence of leucoxene in the chlorite flakes indicates
these have replaced titaniferous mafic precursors such as biotite or amphibole, but no obvious
definitive pseudomorph outlines are present. Brown pleochroism in many of these chlorite flakes
may be indicative of relict biotite. Sparse larger clots of chlorite–leucoxene with rare epidote and
swathed with randomly orientated prismatic carbonate–quartz pseudomorphs, show strong
preferred elongation parallel to the finer groundmass chlorite foliation and may represent former
aggregates of biotite and amphibole or biotite and plagioclase. A few larger prismatic carbonate–
quartz pseudomorphs ≤2 mm long and containing abundant precursor cleavage-orientated
leucoxene and chlorite, resemble altered prismatic amphibole. A few subhedral to anhedral
opaques and rounded apatite granules are disseminated throughout the rock. This sample is
tentatively interpreted to be of an altered, amphibolitized intermediate rock of volcanic origin. The
rock has been metamorphosed, possibly to at least biotite grade of greenschist facies
metamorphism, but strong sericite–chlorite alteration has obliterated critical detail. No zircon was
recognized, but there are a few pleochroic radio-haloes around minute unidentified inclusions in
chlorite flakes.

Zircon morphology
The zircons isolated from this sample are commonly yellow-brown to dark brown or black. A wide
range in shapes are represented and many grains are fractured, but most are euhedral in shape and
between 20 × 40 µm and 60 × 100 µm in size. Most grains display strong internal igneous
zonation.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 4 and 8 November 1999. The counter deadtime during both analysis
sessions was 32 ns. During the first session, two analyses of the CZ3 standard were obtained and a
Pb*/U calibration error of 1.0 (1σ%) was applied. Analyses 1.1 to 6.1 were obtained during the first
session. During the second session, eleven analyses of the CZ3 standard indicated a Pb*/U
calibration error of 2.33 (1σ%). Common-Pb corrections were applied assuming Broken Hill
common-Pb isotopic compositions for all analyses, with the exception of analyses 9.1, 14.1, 17.1
and 19.1, for which isotopic compositions determined using the method of Cumming and Richards
(1975) were assumed.

Results
Twenty-three analyses were obtained from 19 zircons. Results are given in Table 15 and shown on
a concordia plot in Figure 17.

   242634.1: hornblende–biotite metagranodiorite, Koolanooka Hills
                 (Walganna Suite, Bald Rock Supersuite; Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                                SS Romano, MTD Wingate, Y LU, IOH Fielding and TJ Ivanic


Location and sampling
PERENJORI SH50-06, PERENJORI 2139                                                 Zone 50, MGA 423759E 6772980N
Warox Site TJIKNK190050                                                                      Sampled on 3 May 2019
This sample was collected from bouldery outcrop north of the Koolanooka Hills, about 9.7 km southwest of Sandalwood
Bore, 3.7 km south-southeast of Charley Well, and 1.9 km northeast of the Koolanooka open pit mine.

Geological context
The unit sampled is a metagranodiorite of the Walganna Suite (Van Kranendonk et al., 2013; Zibra, 2021: ENS report
Walganna Suite, viewed 31 May 2023) (Fig. 1), within the northern Koolanooka greenstone belt (Baxter and Lipple, 1985).
The 2650–2600 Ma Walganna Suite consists mainly of equigranular, biotite±muscovite monzogranite, locally K-feldspar
porphyritic, that commonly exhibits a magmatic foliation. As one of the youngest units in the Youanmi Terrane,
monzogranite of the widespread Walganna Suite is intruded into older granitic rocks and greenstones of the Murchison and
Southern Cross Supergroups (Zibra, 2021: ENS report Walganna Suite, viewed 31 May 2023; Ivanic et al., 2022). The
Koolanooka greenstone belt consists mainly of greenschist to amphibolite facies volcanic, sedimentary, and ultramafic to
felsic intrusive rocks, and banded iron formation (Baxter and Lipple, 1985). A felsic metavolcanic rock, sampled about 11.2
km to the southeast, yielded an igneous crystallization age of 2961 ± 4 Ma and an inherited zircon component dated at
2994 ± 3 Ma (GSWA 242652, Wingate et al., 2021a). A metamonzogranite collected about 22 km to the east-northeast
yielded an igneous crystallization age of 2946 ± 4 Ma (GSWA 219311, Wingate et al., 2020). A metarhyolite sampled in
the southern Koolanooka greenstone belt, about 26 km to the south-southeast, yielded an igneous crystallization age of
3002 ± 4 Ma (GSWA 242672, Wingate et al., 2021b).

Petrographic description
This sample is a medium-grained, recrystallized, porphyritic hornblende–biotite metagranodiorite (Fig. 1), consisting of
about 35% plagioclase, 25% quartz, 15% K-feldspar, 12–15% hornblende, 8% biotite, 3–4% opaque minerals, and
accessory titanite, apatite and zircon. Plagioclase (An38, andesine) is weakly sericitized, and forms subhedral to euhedral,
                                                              1
tabular phenocrysts up to 5 mm long, as well as subhedral grains up to 0.25 mm in size. Quartz is weakly to moderately
strained, and forms anhedral phenocrysts up to 3 mm diameter, as well as equant, irregular grains up to 0.25 mm in size,
together with feldspars in granoblastic aggregates. Green hornblende is anhedral, tabular, up to 2 mm long, with interior
zones of actinolite and minor magnetite, which may represent altered original pyroxene subsequently rimmed by
hornblende. Some hornblende contains tiny quartz inclusions. Fined-grained glomeroporphyritic aggregates of hornblende,
plagioclase, biotite and magnetite are up to 8 mm in size. Yellow to brown biotite forms anhedral to subhedral laths and
decussate aggregates of subhedral grains up to 0.25 mm long. Magnetite is anhedral, slightly embayed, up to 1 mm in size,
and locally intergrown with or rimmed by titanite. Rhombic titanite crystals up to 0.15 mm in size, and euhedral apatite up
to 0.6 mm long, occur within hornblende-rich aggregates. The groundmass contains patches of fine-grained granophyre up
to 1 mm in size. The sample lacks a tectonic fabric, but is extensively recrystallized, indicating contact metamorphism to
hornblende hornfels facies.

Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are colourless to dark red–brown or opaque, and mainly euhedral. The crystals are up to
500 µm long, and mainly elongate to acicular, with aspect ratios up to 9:1. In cathodoluminescence (CL) images,
concentric zoning is ubiquitous, some zircons contain embayed cores, and many grains contain inclusions. A CL image of
representative zircons is shown in Figure 2.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 24–25 September 2020, using SHRIMP-B. Twelve analyses of the M257 standard were
obtained during the session, of which 10 analyses indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 0.50%
(1σ) and a 238U/206Pb* calibration uncertainty of 0.25% (1σ). Calibration uncertainties are included in the errors of
238U/206Pb* ratios and dates listed in Table 1 (provided in the Links section). Common-Pb corrections were applied to all
analyses using contemporaneous isotopic compositions determined according to the model of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
Eighteen analyses were obtained from 18 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1, and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig. 3).

Interpretation
The analyses are concordant to slightly discordant (Fig. 3), and form a single group, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb* ratios.

Group I comprises 18 analyses (Table 1), which yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 2627 ± 6 Ma (MSWD =
1.2).
The date of 2627 ± 6 Ma for the 18 analyses in Group I is interpreted as the igneous crystallization age.

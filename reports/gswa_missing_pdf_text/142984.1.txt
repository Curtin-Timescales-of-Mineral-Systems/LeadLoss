       142984: pegmatite-banded, even-grained orthogneiss,
                           Talga Well

Location and sampling
NULLAGINE (SF 51–5)                                                          120°01'50"E 21°11'06"S
Sampled on 28 September 1998
The sample was taken from a 1 m diameter block located adjacent to the north side of the Ripon
Hills road and 3 km south-southwest of Talga Well.

Tectonic unit/relations
This sample is from a pegmatite-banded, grey even-grained gneiss of the Mount Edgar Granitoid
Complex. Two obvious phases within the rock — the grey gneiss and white pegmatite phase —
have been deformed to form a grey and white layered rock.

Petrographic description
The principal minerals present in this sample are plagioclase (55–60 vol.%), quartz (35 vol.%),
biotite (5 vol.%), epidote (2–3 vol.%), allanite (trace), titanite (trace), sericite (trace), adularia
(trace), carbonate (trace), epidote (trace), and zircon (trace). This is a foliated, fine-grained
plagioclase-rich rock with K-feldspar highlighted on the stained offcut in veins, and as patches in
and adjacent to plagioclase grains. It is an altered tonalite with accessory titanite and zircon and
veins containing adularia, carbonate, quartz, and rare epidote. There are also veins that lack K-
feldspar. The thin section shows abundant plagioclase as rounded, augen-like grains ≤2 mm in
diameter and less abundant quartz, partly as grains ≤2 mm long and partly as a micromosaic of
smaller recrystallized grains. A foliation is defined by chloritized fine-grained biotite, to 0.8 mm in
grain size. Lenses of epidote ≤3 mm long are mostly elongated parallel to the foliation and locally
enclose allanite grains. Rare titanite ≤1 mm in grain size encloses crystals of zircon ≤100 µm long.
Some zircon also lies within quartz. Some of the plagioclase is dusted with sericite, more rarely
with patches of adularia. Veins consist variably of adularia and/or carbonate and quartz and/or
carbonate with minor epidote. The widest vein is 2 mm thick, with a zone of granular to prismatic
quartz and minor carbonate, passing into a zone of carbonate with less abundant fine-grained
quartz.

Zircon morphology
The zircons isolated from this sample are commonly pale green-brown, dark brown to black,
euhedral with rounded terminations, elongate and between 50 × 90 µm and 250 × 400 µm in size.
Most grains have remnant fine internal zonation but a minority are apparently structureless or have
structureless cores surrounded by strongly zoned margins. Many are black and metamict. Fluid,
mineral, and opaque inclusions are common.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 16 and 22 August 1999. The counter deadtime during both sessions
was 32 ns. During the first session, seven analyses of the CZ3 standard indicated a Pb*/U
calibration error of 1.26 (1σ%). Analyses 1.1 to 21.1 were obtained during the first session. During
the second session, nine analyses of the CZ3 standard indicated a Pb*/U calibration error of
1.64 (1σ%). Common-Pb corrections were applied assuming Broken Hill common-Pb isotopic
compositions for all analyses, with the exception of analysis 7.1, for which isotopic compositions
determined using the method of Cumming and Richards (1975) were assumed.

Results
Twenty-five analyses were obtained from 25 zircons. Results are given in Table 46 and shown on a
concordia plot in Figure 52.

   19.1 and 24.1), assigned to Group 2, have 207Pb/206Pb ratios defining a single population and
   indicating a weighted mean date of 3321 ± 6 Ma (chi-squared = 1.14). Concordant analysis 5.1 and
   discordant analysis 10.1 have lower 207Pb/206Pb ratios than members of these two groups, whereas

   ratios.
        The date of 3321 ± 6 Ma indicated by the weighted mean 207Pb/206Pb ratio of the six analyses of
   Group 2 is interpreted as the time of igneous crystallization of the grey, even-grained phase within
   this sample, whereas the date of 3300 ± 4 Ma indicated by the weighted mean 207Pb/206Pb ratio of
   the fifteen analyses of Group 1 is interpreted as the time of igneous crystallization of the pegmatite
   phase. Analyses 5.1 and 10.1 indicated relatively high U and Th concentrations and the lower

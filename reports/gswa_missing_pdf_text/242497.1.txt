        242497.1: hornblende–biotite metagranodiorite, Mungo Pool
                           (Yilgarn Craton granites; Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                                 SS Romano, MTD Wingate, Y LU, IOH Fielding and TJ Ivanic


Location and sampling
MURGOO SG50-14, MURGOO 2143                                                      Zone 50, MGA 433362E 6987169N
Warox Site TJIMUR190010                                                              Sampled on 3 September 2019
This sample was collected from a bouldery outcrop, about 2.3 km south-southwest of Noolya Pool, 2.1 km west of Mungo
Pool, and 0.7 km north of the Meeberrie–Wooleen Road.

Geological context
The unit sampled is metagranodiorite assigned to Yilgarn Craton granites (Fig.1), near the southeastern margin of the
Barloweerie greenstone belt (Baxter, 1974). A sheet of similar granitic rock, about 0.1 km to the west-southwest, is
intruded into foliated quartzite, a sample of which yielded 3056–3019 Ma detrital zircons and a conservative maximum
depositional age of 3037 ± 2 Ma (GSWA 242498, Romano et al., 2023).

Petrographic description
This sample is an equigranular, medium-grained metagranodiorite (Fig. 2), consisting of about 45–50% plagioclase, 30%
quartz, 10–12% hornblende, 5–10% K-feldspar, 4–5% magnetite, 2% biotite, <1% myrmekite, and trace relict pyroxene
and zircon. Plagioclase is anhedral, equant to tabular, up to 5 mm long, and exhibits tapered deformation twins and
incipient sericite alteration. Quartz is anhedral, irregular, equant to elongate, fractured, up to 4 mm in size, and non-strained
to weakly strained. K-feldspar is anhedral, internally featureless, and bordered by lobes of myrmekite up to 1 mm in
diameter. Parts of the feldspar–quartz aggregate exhibit well-developed polygonal texture. Green hornblende is anhedral,
irregular, and up to 2 mm in size, with smoothly indented margins against adjacent quartz and feldspar. Some hornblende
occurs in aggregates with less abundant biotite and magnetite. Relict clinopyroxene is preserved in the cores of some
hornblende grains. Orange–brown biotite forms anhedral, irregular grains that have replaced some hornblende grains and
fringes magnetite grains. Magnetite forms finely pitted, anhedral grains up to 0.6 mm in size that are enclosed by
hornblende. The polygonal texture and development of myrmekite indicate deformation and partial grain boundary recovery
at moderate metamorphic grade.

Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are pale brown to dark brown or opaque and mainly subhedral to euhedral. The crystals
are up to 300 µm long, and equant to slightly elongate, with aspect ratios up to 3:1. In cathodoluminescence (CL) images,
broad concentric zoning is common, although poorly defined. A CL image of representative zircons is shown in Figure 3.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 5–6 November 2021, using SHRIMP-B. Eleven analyses of the M257 standard were obtained
during the session, of which 10 analyses indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 0.50% (1σ) and a
238U/206Pb* calibration uncertainty of 0.19% (1σ). Calibration uncertainties are included in the errors of 238U/206Pb*
ratios and dates listed in Table 1 (provided in the Links section). Common-Pb corrections were applied to all analyses using
contemporaneous isotopic compositions determined according to the model of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
Twenty analyses were obtained from 20 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1, and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig. 4).

Interpretation
The analyses are concordant to moderately concordant (Fig. 4). One analysis is >5% discordant. The date obtained from
this analysis (Group D; Table 1) is unreliable, and considered not to be geologically significant. The remaining 19 analyses
form a single group, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb* ratios.

Group X comprises 19 analyses (Table 1), which yield 207Pb*/206Pb* dates of 3048–3009 Ma.
The dates of 3048–3009 Ma for the 19 analyses in Group X are interpreted as the ages of inherited zircons. The oldest 18
analyses in Group X yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 3040 ± 3 Ma (MSWD = 0.78), interpreted as the age of
an inherited component. The date of 3040 ± 3 Ma is considered a maximum age for igneous crystallization of the
granodiorite.
Interpretation of the zircons from this sample as interpreted is based on their essentially identical appearance, U and Th
content, and age as those isolated from an adjacent quartzite (single component at 3037 ± 2 Ma, GSWA 242498, Romano
et al., 2023) thought to be intruded by the granodiorite.

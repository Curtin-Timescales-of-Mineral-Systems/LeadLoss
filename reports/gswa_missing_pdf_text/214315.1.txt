                  214315.1: metagranodiorite dyke, Wooley Spring
                (Big Bell Suite, Austin Downs Supersuite; Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                                       IOH Fielding, Y Lu, MTD Wingate and I Zibra


Location and sampling
YALGOO SH50-02, BADJA 2240                                                          Zone 50, MGA 467528E 6825120N
Warox Site MZFYAD000121                                                                     Sampled on 21 May 2014
This sample was collected from low outcrop on the west side of a fenceline track on Bunnawarra Station, about 5.2 km
east-northeast of New Bore, 4.3 km west-northwest of Brimson Well, and 0.6 km north of Wooley Spring.

Geological context
The unit sampled is a metagranodiorite dyke assigned to the 2732–2686 Ma Big Bell Suite of the Austin Downs Supersuite
(Van Kranendonk et al., 2013; Zibra, 2021: ENS Report Big Bell Suite, viewed 28 April 2023). The Big Bell Suite mainly
consists of K-feldspar porphyritic and equigranular monzogranite and includes two north-trending batholiths exposed on
either flank of the northeast-trending Meekatharra–Wydgee greenstone belt (Zibra, 2021: ENS Report Big Bell Suite,
viewed 28 April 2023). At this locality, within the core of the Yalgoo dome, the dyke is north trending and crosscuts the
foliation in the Kynea Migmatite of the Bunnawarra Suite (Fig. 1), a sample of which collected 1.5 km to the south yielded
an igneous crystallization age of 2960 ± 10 Ma (GSWA 209689, Lu et al., 2017). The sample contains biotite-rich schlieren
and inclusions of tonalite that are compositionally and microstructurally comparable to those from sample GSWA 209689
(Zibra et al., 2018). A metasyenogranite dyke of the Big Bell Suite sampled about 2.2 km to the south yielded an igneous
crystallization age of 2705 ± 6 Ma (GSWA 214324, Lu et al., 2020).

Petrographic description
The sample is a medium-grained metagranodiorite dyke (Fig. 1), consisting of about 40% plagioclase, 30% quartz, 15–20%
K-feldspar, 5% biotite, 2–3% myrmekite, <1% opaques and accessory apatite and possible metamict allanite. Plagioclase is
slightly sericitized, and mostly occurs as equant, anhedral grains up to 3 mm in size. Smaller xenoblastic plagioclase form
polygonal and semipolygonal aggregates. Weakly strained quartz occurs as irregular fractured grains up to 4 mm in size.
                                                             1
The fine fractures appear to delineate subgrains and new grains <1 mm in size. Quartz forms small, rounded inclusions in
larger plagioclase and K-feldspar grains. K-feldspar is finely microperthitic, equant, anhedral and up to 2.5 mm in size, and
mostly exhibits fine microcline twinning. Myrmekite lobes up to 1 mm across occur on K-feldspar grain boundaries and
within polygonal aggregates of smaller grains. Biotite occurs as randomly oriented, anhedral to subhedral laths up to 1.5
mm long, and is partly replaced by chlorite and trace muscovite. Probable magnetite, occurs as equant subhedral grains up
to 0.2 mm in diameter. Allanite forms pale brown metamict tabular grains up to 0.4 mm long and apatite occurs as rare
euhedral laths to 0.1 mm long. The sample lacks a tectonic foliation but contains evidence that it has been deformed and has
undergone considerable solid-state grain boundary recovery at moderate metamorphic grade.


Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are colourless to dark brown and subhedral to euhedral. The crystals are up to 450 µm
long and elongate with aspect ratios up to 6:1. In cathodoluminescence (CL) images, concentric zoning is ubiquitous. Many
crystals contain high uranium, metamict zones. A CL image of representative zircons is shown in Figure 2.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 19–20 May 2015, using SHRIMP-B. Eleven analyses of the BR266 standard obtained during
the session indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 0.50% (1σ) and a 238U/206Pb* calibration
uncertainty of 0.19% (1σ). Isotopic mass fractionation of 207Pb*/206Pb* ratios during the session was corrected by
reference to the OGC1 standard; measured ratios were increased by 0.44%. Calibration uncertainties are included in the
errors of 238U/206Pb* ratios and dates listed in Table 1 (provided in the Links section). Common-Pb corrections were
applied to all analyses using contemporaneous isotopic compositions determined according to the model of Stacey and
Kramers (1975).

Results
Eighteen analyses were obtained from 18 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1 and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig. 3).

Interpretation
The analyses are concordant to moderately discordant (Fig. 3), and form two groups, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb* ratios.

Group I comprises 17 analyses of 17 zircons (Table 1), which yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 2700 ± 7 Ma
(MSWD = 1.8).

Group X comprises a single analysis of a zircon core (Table 1), which yields a 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 2958 ± 20 Ma (1σ).
The date of 2700 ± 7 Ma for the 17 analyses in Group I is interpreted as the magmatic crystallization age of the
metagranodiorite dyke. The date of 2958 ± 20 Ma (1σ) for the analysis in Group X is interpreted as the age of an inherited
component.

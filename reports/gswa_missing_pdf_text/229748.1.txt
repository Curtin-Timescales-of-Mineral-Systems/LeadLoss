                      229748.1: tonalitic gneiss, New Holland Mine
                           (Yilgarn Craton granites; Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                                   SS Romano, Y Lu, IOH Fielding, MTD Wingate and I Zibra


Location and sampling
SIR SAMUEL SG51-13, DEPOT SPRINGS 2942                                            Zone 51, MGA 250032E 6900690N
Warox Site IXZDPS000935                                                                  Sampled on 8 October 2017
This sample was collected from low outcrop on Pinnacles Station, about 5.6 km north-northeast of Five Mile Well, 3.5 km
west of the New Holland mine open pits, and 2.8 km due south of the Sandstone – Agnew Road.

Geological context
The unit sampled is a tonalitic gneiss (Fig. 1) in the hanging wall of the c. 2660 Ma Waroonga Shear Zone (WSZ), along
the eastern margin of the Youanmi Terrane (Zibra et al., 2017). The Youanmi Terrane is composed of Meso- to
Neoarchean granite-greenstone lithologies that range in age from c. 3.3 Ga to c. 2.72 Ga and includes gneissic-granitic
rocks intruded c. 3.0 Ga to c. 2.6 Ga. The WSZ is a west-dipping, low-angle, listric structure that postdates regional east-
dipping fabrics (Zibra et al., 2017). A c. 2660 Ma syntectonic monzogranite gneiss sampled about 0.7 km to the north-
northeast only yielded xenocrystic zircons dated at 2849–2781 Ma with two main inherited components of 2820 ± 3 and
2802 ± 6 Ma (GSWA 212791; Wingate et al. 2021).

Petrographic description
The sample is a weakly foliated, fine-grained tonalitic gneiss, consisting of about 55% plagioclase, 35% quartz, 6–7%
biotite, 3–4% muscovite and accessory zircon. Plagioclase occurs xenoblastic, slightly elongate grains up to 3 mm in size
with sharp, finely serrated boundaries with other plagioclase grains and smoothly lobate boundaries with quartz grains;
twining is rare and where it occurs is very faint and finely spaced. Quartz occurs as xenoblastic, slightly elongate grains
<1.5 mm in size between plagioclase grains, or as very small (<0.2 mm in diameter) round inclusions within plagioclase.
Brown biotite occurs as sub-idioblastic grains to 1 mm in length. Muscovite forms idioblastic and sub-idioblastic laths to 1
mm in length, although most are <0.5 mm. Individual laths and small folia of both micas define the wavy tectonic foliation,
and parallel the slightly elongate plagioclase and quartz. The only observed accessory mineral are rare zircons to 0.05 mm
slender prismatic grains. The sample has been deformed and undergone grain boundary adjustment at relatively high grade
to form a low interfacial free energy, semi-polygonal aggregate.

Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are colourless to dark brown or opaque, mainly subhedral to euhedral, and slightly
rounded. The crystals are up to 500 µm long, and mostly elongate, with aspect ratios up to 7:1. In cathodoluminescence
(CL) images, concentric zoning is ubiquitous, and many crystals contain high uranium, metamict zones. A CL image of
representative zircons is shown in Figure 2.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 21–22 October 2021, using SHRIMP-B. Fifteen analyses of the M257 standard were obtained
during the session, of which 11 analyses indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 0.80% (1σ) and a
238U/206Pb*calibration uncertainty of 0.32% (1σ). Calibration uncertainties are included in the errors of 238U/206Pb*ratios
and dates listed in Table 1 (provided in the Links section). Common-Pb corrections were applied to all analyses using
contemporaneous isotopic compositions determined according to the model of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
Seventeen analyses were obtained from 15 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1 and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig. 3).

Interpretation
The analyses are concordant to strongly discordant (Fig. 3). Six analyses are >6% discordant. The dates obtained from
these six analyses (Group D; Table 1) are unreliable, and considered not to be geologically significant. The remaining 11
analyses can be divided into two groups, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb* ratios.

Group I comprises nine analyses of eight zircons (Table 1), which yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 2821 ± 10
Ma (MSWD = 1.4).

Group P comprises two analyses of two zircons (Table 1), which yield 207Pb*/206Pb* dates of 2782 ± 13 and 2781 ± 12
Ma (1 σ).

The date of 2821 ± 10 Ma for the nine analyses in Group I is interpreted as the igneous crystallization age of the tonalitic
gneiss. The dates of 2782 ± 13 and 2781 ± 12 Ma (1 σ) for the two analyses in Group P are younger than the second
analysis from the same grain and are interpreted to reflect loss of radiogenic Pb.

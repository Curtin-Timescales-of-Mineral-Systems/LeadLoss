                     242491.1: metamonzogranite, Piabubba Well
                (Big Bell Suite, Austin Downs Supersuite; Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                               SS Romano, MTD Wingate, Y LU, IOH Fielding and TJ Ivanic


Location and sampling
BYRO SG50-10, BOOLARDY 2244                                                       Zone 50, MGA 482270E 7029601N
Warox Site TJIKAL190117                                                                Sampled on 3 September 2019
This sample was collected from the northern edge of a 150 m-wide outcrop on Kalli Station, about 6.1 km south of
Cardewandabubba Well, 4.2 km west-northwest of Wandarie Well, and 2.0 km north-northeast of Piabubba Well.

Geological context
The unit sampled is a metamonzogranite (Fig. 1) of the 2732–2686 Ma Big Bell Suite of the Austin Downs Supersuite (Van
Kranendonk et al., 2013). The Big Bell Suite consists mainly of porphyritic and equigranular metagranodiorite to
metamonzogranite, which commonly exhibits a magmatic foliation (Zibra, 2021: ENS report Big Bell Suite, viewed 3
August 2023). A metamonzogranite sampled about 21 km to the east yielded an igneous crystallization age of 2693 ± 7 Ma
(GSWA 242490, Romano et al., 2023). Several granitic samples collected about 36 km to the north yielded igneous
crystallization ages of 2717–2680 Ma (e.g. GSWA 105017, Nelson, 1996). A tonalite gneiss collected about 107 km to the
southeast yielded an igneous crystallization age of 2724 ± 2 Ma (GSWA 185923, Wingate et al., 2011).

Petrographic description
The sample is an inequigranular, coarse-grained metamonzogranite, consisting of about 35–40% K-feldspar, 30% quartz,
25% plagioclase, 4% myrmekite, 3% biotite, <1% magnetite and accessory allanite and zircon. K-feldspar is subhedral,
equant to tabular, microperthitic and up to 10 mm long. The larger grains contain tabular inclusions of plagioclase up to 1
mm in size, and many are fringed with lobes of myrmekite up to 0.3 mm in diameter. Quartz forms anhedral, irregular,
recrystallized aggregates up to 7 mm long, in which individual grains are non-strained or weakly strained. Plagioclase is
mainly anhedral and up to 7 mm in size. The rock contains patches of fine-grained, recrystallized plagioclase, mymekite, K-
feldspar and quartz with polygonal texture. Green–brown biotite forms ragged, anhedral to subhedral laths up to 2 mm
long, in part aligned in poorly defined, discontinuous folia. Magnetite forms small equant grains and irregular, elongate

grains up to 1 mm long. Allanite is anhedral, metamict, and up to 0.15 mm diameter. The sample exhibits a weak tectonic
foliation. Deformation and grain-boundary recovery occurred at moderate metamorphic grade.

Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are pale violet to dark brown and mainly subhedral to euhedral. The crystals are up to 350
µm long, and equant to elongate, with aspect ratios up to 6:1. In cathodoluminescence (CL) images, concentric zoning is
ubiquitous, many crystals are dominated by high-U, metamict zones, and some appear to contain older cores. A CL image
of representative zircons is shown in Figure 2.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 28–29 October 2021, using SHRIMP-B. Eleven analyses of the M257 standard were obtained
during the session, of which nine analyses indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 0.70% (1σ) and
a 238U/206Pb* calibration uncertainty of 0.28% (1σ). Isotopic mass fractionation of 207Pb/206Pb ratios during the session
was corrected by reference to the OGC1 standard; measured ratios were decreased by 0.22%. Calibration uncertainties are
included in the errors of 238U/206Pb* ratios and dates listed in Table 1 (provided in the Links section). Common-Pb
corrections were applied to all analyses using contemporaneous isotopic compositions determined according to the model
of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
Twenty-one analyses were obtained from 21 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1, and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig.
3).

Interpretation
The analyses are concordant to moderately discordant (Fig. 3). Two analyses are >5% discordant. The dates obtained from
these two analyses (Group D; Table 1) are unreliable and are considered not to be geologically significant. The remaining
19 analyses can be divided into two groups, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb* ratios and their positions within the crystals.

Group I comprises 17 analyses (Table 1), which yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 2701 ± 5 Ma (MSWD =
1.4).

Group X comprises two analyses of zircon cores (Table 1), which yield 207Pb*/206Pb* dates of c. 3296 and 3247 Ma.
The date of 2701 ± 5 Ma for the 17 analyses in Group I is interpreted as the magmatic crystallization age of the
monzogranite. The dates of c. 3296 and 3247 Ma for the two analyses in Group X are interpreted as the ages of inherited
zircons.

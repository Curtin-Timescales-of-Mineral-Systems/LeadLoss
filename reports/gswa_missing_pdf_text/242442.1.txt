                 242442.1: feldspathic gneiss, Passchendeele farm
                           (Thundelarra Supersuite; Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                                SS Romano, MTD Wingate, Y LU, IOH Fielding and TJ Ivanic


Location and sampling
PERENJORI SH50-06, CARNAMAH 2038                                                     Zone 50, MGA 398957E 6728666N
Warox Site TJIROT190061                                                                      Sampled on 15 May 2019
This sample was collected from outcrop along the Carnamah – Perenjori Road, about 11.4 km southeast of Three Springs
talc mine, 5.1 km east-northeast of Inering Hill, and 3.6 km west of Passchendeele farm.

Geological context
The unit sampled is a feldspathic gneiss (Fig. 1) of the Thundelarra Supersuite (Ivanic et al., 2022). The 3010–2930 Ma
Thundelarra Supersuite consists mainly of migmatitic tonalite to granodiorite gneiss, and is the oldest basement unit in the
Youanmi Terrane, coeval with the oldest recognized felsic metavolcanic rocks of the Southern Cross Supergroup, including
the Wongan, Madoonga, Gossan Hill, Mount Gibson, Honman, and Annabelle Volcanics Formations (Van Kranendonk et
al., 2013; Ivanic et al., 2022). Felsic metavolcanic rocks 44–49 km to the northeast, in the Koolanooka greenstone belt,
yielded igneous crystallization ages of 3002 ± 4 Ma (GSWA 242672, Wingate et al., 2021b) and 2961 ± 4 Ma (GSWA
242652, Wingate et al., 2021a). A metamonzogranite sampled about 69 km to the northeast yielded an igneous
crystallization age of 2946 ± 4 Ma (GSWA 219311, Wingate et al., 2020).

Petrographic description
The sample is a foliated, fine to medium-grained feldspathic gneiss (Fig. 1), consisting of about 43% plagioclase, 32%
quartz, 17% K-feldspar, 6–7% biotite, 2% myrmekite, <1% magnetite and accessory allanite, apatite and zircon.
Plagioclase is subhedral to anhedral, equant, up to 3 mm in size, and variably sericitized. Quartz is anhedral, irregular, up to
3 mm in size, and exhibits undulose extinction and minor subgraining. The larger quartz grains have coarsely intergrown
margins with adjacent feldspar. K-feldspar is microperthitic and forms simply twinned phenocrysts up to at least 9 mm long,
as well as anhedral, interstitial grains up to 1 mm in size, some of which exhibit microcline twinning. Curved boundaries
between many quartz and feldspar grains indicate grain boundary adjustment during metamorphism and deformation. Lobes
of myrmekite up to 1 mm long have partially replaced some K-feldspar grains. Biotite forms slender, subhedral laths up to
                                                               1
1.5 mm long and small, aligned decussate aggregates that define the foliation. Equant subhedral magnetite is up to 0.7 mm
across, and metamict allanite is subhedral to euhedral and up to 0.8 mm in diameter. Apatite is subhedral, tabular and up to
0.4 mm. Deformation produced the biotite foliation and strained quartz and feldspar and was followed by recovery at
moderate metamorphic grade.

Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are pale brown to dark brown or opaque, subhedral to euhedral, and variably rounded.
The crystals are up to 400 µm long, and equant to elongate, with aspect ratios up to 6:1. In cathodoluminescence (CL)
images, concentric zoning is ubiquitous, and many crystals contain high-U, metamict zones and inclusions. A CL image of
representative zircons is shown in Figure 2.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 15–16 October 2021, using SHRIMP-B. Ten analyses of the M257 standard obtained during
the session indicated an external spot-to spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 0.50% (1σ) and a 238U/206Pb* calibration
uncertainty of 0.28% (1σ). Calibration uncertainties are included in the errors of 238U/206Pb* ratios and dates listed in
Table 1 (provided in the Links section). Common-Pb corrections were applied to all analyses using contemporaneous
isotopic compositions determined according to the model of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
Eighteen analyses were obtained from 18 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1, and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig. 3).

Interpretation
The analyses are concordant to moderately discordant (Fig. 3). One analysis is >5% discordant. The date obtained from this
analysis (Group D; Table 1) is unreliable and is considered not to be geologically significant. The remaining 17 analyses
form two groups, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb* ratios.
                                                              2
Group I comprises 13 analyses (Table 1), which yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 2939 ± 5 Ma (MSWD =
1.2).

Group P comprises four analyses (Table 1), which yield 207Pb*/206Pb* dates of 2915–2851 Ma.
The date of 2939 ± 5 Ma for the 13 analyses in Group I is interpreted as the magmatic crystallization age of the
granodiorite. The dates of 2915–2851 Ma for the four analyses in Group P are interpreted to reflect ancient loss of
radiogenic Pb.

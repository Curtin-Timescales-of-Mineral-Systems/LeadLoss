                                   242498.1: quartzite, Mungo Pool
                                          (Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                                SS Romano, MTD Wingate, Y LU, IOH Fielding and TJ Ivanic


Location and sampling
MURGOO SG50-14, MURGOO 2143                                                       Zone 50, MGA 433317E 6987137N
Warox Site TJIMUR190009                                                               Sampled on 3 September 2019
This sample was collected from a north-trending outcrop, about 2.4 km south-southwest of Noolya Pool, 2.1 km west of
Mungo Pool, and 0.6 km north of the Meeberrie–Wooleen Road.

Geological context
The unit sampled is a quartzite (Fig. 1) near the southeastern margin of the Barloweerie greenstone belt (Baxter, 1974).
About 0.1 km to the east-northeast, the quartzite is intruded by a metagranodiorite sheet, which contains 3048–3009 Ma
zircon xenocrysts that are essentially identical in appearance, isotopic characteristics, and age to those in the quartzite
(GSWA 242497, Romano et al., 2023).

Petrographic description
This sample is a fine-grained, quartz–muscovite rock (Fig. 1) crosscut by quartz veinlets. The quartz–muscovite parts of the
sample consist of about 55% quartz, 45% muscovite and trace rutile. Quartz is equant, unstrained, and up to 0.1 mm in
diameter. Muscovite laths are subhedral and randomly oriented, and rutile forms trails and small clusters of anhedral crystals
up to 0.05 mm long. Quartz grains form a semi-polygonal aggregate with each other and with muscovite laths. The sample
contains parallel boudinaged lenses up to 4 mm wide and smaller pods composed of coarsely microcrystalline, unstrained
quartz. The lenses and pods are interpreted as remnants of dismembered, tightly folded quartz veins. The quartz–muscovite
parts of the sample may represent a metamorphosed quartz-rich siltstone, although the absence of obvious detrital grain
shapes in quartz and of sedimentary structures such as bedding, suggest, together with the presence of vein quartz, that the
sample could have a hydrothermal origin.

Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are pale brown to dark brown and mainly subhedral to euhedral. The crystals are up to

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 16–17 September 2021 using SHRIMP-B. Ten analyses of the M257 standard were obtained
during the session, of which nine analyses indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 0.50% (1σ) and
a 238U/206Pb* calibration uncertainty of 0.18% (1σ). Calibration uncertainties are included in the errors of 238U/206Pb*
ratios and dates listed in Table 1 (provided in the Links section). Common-Pb corrections were applied to all analyses using
contemporaneous isotopic compositions determined according to the model of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
Forty-two analyses were obtained from 41 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1, and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig. 3).

Interpretation
The analyses are concordant to moderately discordant (Fig. 3). Five analyses are >5% discordant. The dates obtained from
these five analyses (Group D; Table 1) are unreliable, and are considered not to be geologically significant. The remaining
37 analyses can be divided into two groups, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb* ratios.

Group Y comprises one analysis (Table 1), which yields a 207Pb/206Pb* date of 3019 ± 8 Ma (1σ).

Group S comprises 36 analyses of 36 zircons (Table 1), which yield 207Pb/206Pb* dates of 3056–3019 Ma.
It is possible that all of the analyses in Group Y and S are of unmodified detrital zircons, in which case the date of 3019 ± 8
Ma (1σ) for the single analysis in Group Y represents a maximum age of deposition for the sedimentary precursor. A more
conservative estimate of the maximum depositional age can be based on the weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 3037 ±
2 Ma (MSWD = 1.2) for all 37 analyses in Groups Y and S.

                    242493.1: metagranodiorite dyke, Reserve Well
                              (Tuckanarra Suite; Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                                SS Romano, MDT Wingate, Y LU, IOH Fielding and TJ Ivanic


Location and sampling
MURGOO SG50-14, MURGOO 2143                                                        Zone 50, MGA 441558E 7003242N
Warox Site TJIMUR190001                                                               Sampled on 3 September 2019
This sample was collected near the western edge of a 200 m-wide outcrop east of the Beringarra – Pindar Road, about 5.4
km east-northeast of Mount Nicolay, 4.6 km west of Little Well, and 1.6 km south-southeast of Reserve Well.

Geological context
The unit sampled is a 0.5 m-wide, foliated metagranodiorite dyke (Fig.1) assigned to the Tuckanarra Suite (Zibra, 2019:
ENS report Tuckanarra Suite, viewed 24 May 2023). The 2697–2657 Ma Tuckanarra Suite consists of strongly foliated
and locally magmatically layered rocks, with compositions ranging from granodiorite to monzogranite. Most Tuckanarra
Suite rocks consist of coarse-grained, K-feldspar-phyric, biotite metamonzogranite and equigranular monzogranite. Minor
components, which typically form dykes or small intrusions, include quartz diorite, tonalite, granodiorite and muscovite-
bearing leucogranite (Zibra, 2019: ENS report Tuckanarra Suite, viewed 24 May 2023). The dyke at this locality crosscuts
granitic gneiss, assigned to the Annean Supersuite, that yielded an igneous crystallization age of 2741 ± 4 Ma (GSWA
242492, Romano et al. 2023).

Petrographic description
This sample is a fine- to medium-grained metagranodiorite (Fig. 2), which consists of about 45% plagioclase, 30% quartz,
15–18% K-feldspar, 5% biotite, 2% magnetite, <1% allanite and accessory apatite, pyrite, hematite and zircon. Plagioclase
is mainly anhedral, equant, up to 3 mm in size, and altered to sericite and muscovite. Quartz is anhedral, elongate,
fractured, up to 4 mm long, and exhibits weak undulose extinction and lobate grain boundaries. K-feldspar is anhedral,
equant, microperthitic, up to 0.7 mm in size, and exhibits microcline twinning. Brown biotite forms subhedral laths up to 1
mm long that are partially replaced by chlorite and define an undulating foliation. Magnetite is subhedral, pitted, and up to
0.7 mm in diameter. Subhedral pyrite is up to 0.4 mm in size and rimmed with hematite. Allanite is anhedral to subhedral,
equant, metamict, and up to 0.5 mm in size. Euhedral apatite is equant to tabular and up to 0.2 mm long. The sample has

Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are pale brown to dark brown or violet, subhedral to euhedral, and variably rounded. The
crystals are up to 450 µm long, and equant to elongate, with aspect ratios up to 6:1. In cathodoluminescence (CL) images,
concentric zoning is ubiquitous, and many crystals contain embayed cores. A CL image of representative zircons is shown
in Figure 3.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 28–29 October 2021, using SHRIMP-B. Eleven analyses of the M257 standard were obtained
during the session, of which nine analyses indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 0.70% (1s) and
a 238U/206Pb* calibration uncertainty of 0.28% (1s). Isotopic mass fractionation of 207Pb/206Pb ratios during the first
session was corrected by reference to the OGC1 standard; measured ratios were increased by 0.22%. Calibration
uncertainties are included in the errors of 238U/206Pb* ratios and dates listed in Table 1 (provided in the Links section).
Common-Pb corrections were applied to all analyses using contemporaneous isotopic compositions determined according
to the model of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
Eighteen analyses were obtained from 18 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig. 4).

Interpretation
The analyses are concordant to moderately discordant (Fig. 4), and form a single group, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb*
ratios.

Group I comprises 18 analyses (Table 1), which yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 2687 ± 4 Ma (MSWD =
0.64).
The date of 2687 ± 4 Ma for the 18 analyses in Group I is interpreted as the age of igneous crystallization.

                  229285.1: actinolite–biotite schist, Mount Rupert
                       (Southern Cross Supergroup; Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                               SS Romano, Y Lu, IOH Fielding, MTD Wingate and TJ Ivanic


Location and sampling
MOORA SH50-10, WONGAN 2236                                                           Zone 50, MGA 465084E 6596128N
Warox Site TJISWT000004                                                                    Sampled on 24 August 2020
This sample was collected from the 239.15 – 240.60 m depth interval of diamond drillcore MRRC0024, drilled by Red
River Resources Ltd at their Wongan Hills project. The drillhole is located about 11.5 km west of the Northam Pithara
Road, 6.6 km south-southwest of Little Wongan Hill, and 1.2 km north of Mount Rupert.

Geological context
The unit sampled is an amphibole–biotite schist from the Wongan Hill greenstone belt, Southern Cross Supergroup in the
Youanmi Terrane of the Yilgarn Craton (Quentin de Gromard et al., 2021; Ivanic et al., 2022). The Southern Cross
Supergroup consists of metamorphosed basalt, andesite–dacite–rhyolite, and variable proportions of sedimentary rocks
exposed in several belts along a northwest-trending, 750 km-long corridor from Ravensthorpe in the south to the Weld
Range in the north within the Youanmi Terrane (Ivanic et al., 2022). This supergroup contain six formations, i.e. the
Wongan, Madoonga, Gossan Hill, Mount Gibson, Honman, and Annabelle Volcanics Formations, which are unconformably
overlain by the c. 2.8 Ga Norie and Polelle Groups of the Murchison Supergroup (Van Kranendonk et al., 2013; Ivanic et
al., 2022). This sample yielded a maximum depositional age of 3006 ± 6 Ma (see below), which suggests a stratigraphic
position at the bottom of the Southern Cross Supergroup. A metamonzogranitic dyke sampled about 16.9 km to the south
yielded an igneous crystallization age of 2670 ± 7 Ma with 3219–2785 Ma for inherited zircon cores (GSWA 205933,
Wingate et al 2018). Pidgeon et al. (1990) reported an age of 3010 ± 7 Ma for crystallization of a porphyritic felsic rock
within the Wongan Hills belt. These authors also reported an age of 2651 ± 4 Ma for post-tectonic porphyritic granite east
of the belt, and age components of c. 3.0 and 2.8 Ga for zircons within granitic gneiss south of the belt.

Petrographic description
The sample is a foliated and compositionally layered, actinolite–biotite schist, consisting of about 45–50% quartz, 35%
actinolite, 15% biotite, 3% magnetite, <1% sulfides and accessory plagioclase, apatite and zircon. Compositional layering is
                                                              1
defined by biotite-rich layers up to 7 mm wide with average quartz grain size of ~0.25 mm, and biotite-poor domains with
smaller quartz grains (~0.1 mm). Quartz occurs as xenoblastic and elongate grains up to 0.5 mm in length. Amphibole is
colourless to pale green, subhedral, and elongate to 2 mm in length. Subhedral brown biotite (<1 mm) is strongly aligned
within the foliation. Magnetite occurs as elongated or equant, subhedral grains to 0.2 mm long. Sulfides, dominated by
chalcopyrite and traces of pyrite and pyrrhotite, form clusters (<0.3 mm) or rare lenses (<1 mm) that are aligned parallel to
the foliation. Apatite occurs as bladed euhedral laths (0.15 mm). Metamorphic grade is amphibolite facies and the protolith
is considered to be a thinly bedded Fe–Mg-rich sedimentary rock.

Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are colourless to pale brown, anhedral to subhedral, and variably rounded. The crystals
are up to 300 µm long with aspect ratios up to 3:1. In cathodoluminescence (CL) images, concentric or planar zoning is
ubiquitous, and few grains show discordant or metamict cores. A CL image of zircons is shown in Figure 2.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 22–23 October 2021, using SHRIMP-B. Eight analyses of the M257 standard were obtained
during the session, of which seven analyses indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 0.50% (1σ)
and a 238U/206Pb* calibration uncertainty of 0.27% (1σ). Calibration uncertainties are included in the errors of
238U/206Pb* ratios and dates listed in Table 1 (provided in the Links section). Common-Pb corrections were applied to all
analyses using contemporaneous isotopic compositions determined according to the model of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
Twenty-seven analyses were obtained from 26 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1 and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig.
3) and a probability density plot (Fig. 4).

Interpretation
The analyses are concordant to moderately discordant (Fig. 3). One analysis is >5% discordant. The date obtained from this
analysis (Group D; Table 1) is unreliable and considered not to be geologically significant. The remaining 26 analyses can
be divided into three groups, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb* ratios.

Group Y comprises one analysis (Table 1), which yields a 207Pb*/206Pb date of 2998 ± 18 Ma (1σ).

Group S comprises 23 analyses of 23 zircons (Table 1), which yield 207Pb*/206Pb dates of 3295–2999 Ma.

Group Z comprises two analyses of two zircons (Table 1), which yield 207Pb*/206Pb dates of 2635–2587 Ma.
It is possible that all of the analyses in Groups Y and S are of unmodified detrital zircons, in which case the date of 2998 ±
18 Ma (1σ) for the single analysis in Group Y represents a maximum depositional age for the sedimentary protolith. A more
conservative estimate of the maximum depositional age can be based on the weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb date of 3006 ± 6
Ma (MSWD = 1.2) for the youngest eight analyses in Groups Y and S.
The data for combined Groups Y and S indicate significant age components at c. 3035 and 3005 Ma, based on
contributions from approximately 15 and eight analyses, respectively (Fig. 4). These are interpreted as the ages of zircon-
crystallizing rocks in the detrital source region(s), or as the ages of detrital components within sediments that have been
reworked into this rock.
The dates of 2635–2587 Ma for the two analyses in Group Z are interpreted to indicate processing contaminants because
they are similar in age and Th/U ratios to those from the other sample (GSWA 248284, Fielding et al., 2022) in the same
mount.

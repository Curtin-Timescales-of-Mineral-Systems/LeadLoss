                  242683.1: metarhyolite, Golden Stream open pit
  (Meekatharra Formation, Polelle Group, Murchison Supergroup; Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                                      MTD Wingate, Y Lu, IOH Fielding and TJ Ivanic


Location and sampling
YALGOO SH50-02, MELLENBYE 2140                                                    Zone 50, MGA 440729E 6831071N
Warox Site TJIGUL190012                                                                      Sampled on 6 May 2019
This sample was collected from an outcrop southwest of the Cagacaroon Hills, about 5.5 km south of Armstrong Well, 2.2
km north of Gearless Well, and 0.2 km northeast of the Golden Stream open pit.

Geological context
The unit sampled is a metarhyolite (Fig. 1) of the Meekatharra Formation of the Polelle Group. The 2800–2760 Ma
Meekatharra Formation is about 2.5 km thick and composed mainly of weakly metamorphosed basalt and komatiitic basalt,
with thin interflow felsic metavolcanic and metasedimentary rocks (Van Kranendonk et al., 2013, and references therein).
The Meekatharra Formation conformably overlies the Coodardy Pool Formation at the base of the 2800–2735 Ma Polelle
Group and is conformably overlain by the Greensleeves or Wilgie Mia Formations. The present sample was collected near
the northwestern margin of the Gullewa greenstone belt, west of the Yalgoo Dome (Zibra et al., 2018), from a north-
trending unit of laminated metarhyolite, about 1 m thick, between metabasalt to the west and metavolcaniclastic rocks to
the east. About 3.4 km to the south-southwest, a hydrothermally altered volcaniclastic breccia yielded zircon age
components at c. 2967, 2757, 2731, 2700 and 2671 Ma (GSWA 211120, Wingate et al., 2021), although the youngest age
component is based on dates from high-U zircons and now thought likely to reflect Pb loss associated with hydrothermal
alteration. A hornblende metamonzonite sampled about 4.2 km to the west yielded an igneous crystallization age of 2754 ±
4 Ma (GSWA 242695, Wingate et al., 2022).

Petrographic description
The sample is a weakly laminated, fine- to medium-grained metarhyolite (Fig. 1), consisting of about 80% microcrystalline
groundmass, 15–20% biotite, and 2–3% iron oxide minerals. The groundmass is assumed to consist mainly of quartz,
because no feldspar was identified. The groundmass has an average grain size of about 20 µm, although a minor amount of
dispersed, subangular quartz grains are up to 0.1 mm in size. Subhedral laths of brown biotite are up to 0.1 mm in size,
                                                              1
although most are 0.05 mm or smaller. Slightly larger laths are concentrated in decussate aggregates that form equant
patches up to 0.5 mm diameter and minor lenses up to 1 mm long. The lamination is poorly defined by patches and lenses,
up to 1 mm long, of very fine-grained orange–brown iron oxide minerals, and to a much lesser extent by slight variations in
grain size. The sample is not obviously deformed. Biotite is interpreted as metamorphic, and the lack of preferred
orientation suggests it may be the product of contact metamorphism at biotite grade.

Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are colourless to dark brown or opaque and subhedral to euhedral. The crystals are up to
150 µm long, and equant to slightly elongate, with aspect ratios up to 3:1. In cathodoluminescence (CL) images, concentric
zoning is ubiquitous. A CL image of representative zircons is shown in Figure 2.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed over three sessions, on 20–21 March, 26–27 March, and 14–15 May, 2020, using SHRIMP-B.
Analyses 1.1 to 14.1 (spot numbers 1–14) were obtained during the first session, together with 12 analyses of the M257
standard, of which 11 analyses indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 1.2% (1σ) and a
238U/206Pb* calibration uncertainty of 0.42% (1σ). Analyses 15.1 to 22.1 (spot numbers 15–26) were obtained during the
second session, together with 11 analyses of the M257 standard, of which eight analyses indicated an external spot-to-spot
(reproducibility) uncertainty of 2.33% (1σ) and a 238U/206Pb* calibration uncertainty of 0.99% (1σ). Isotopic mass
fractionation of 207Pb/206Pb ratios during the second session was corrected by reference to the OGC1 standard; measured
ratios were decreased by 0.44%. Analyses 1.3 to 28.1 (spot numbers 27–34) were obtained during the third session,
together with 10 analyses of the M257 standard, which indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of
0.50% (1σ) and a 238U/206Pb* calibration uncertainty of 0.20% (1σ). Isotopic mass fractionation of 207Pb/206Pb ratios
during the third session was corrected by reference to the OGC1 standard; measured ratios were decreased by 0.54%.
Calibration uncertainties are included in the errors of 238U/206Pb* ratios and dates listed in Table 1 (provided in the Links
section). Common-Pb corrections were applied to all analyses using contemporaneous isotopic compositions determined
according to the model of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
Thirty-four analyses were obtained from 28 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1 and shown in concordia diagrams (Figs 3
and 4).

Interpretation
The analyses are concordant to strongly discordant (Fig. 3). Eleven analyses are >5% discordant. The dates obtained from
these 11 analyses (Group D; Table 1) are unreliable and are considered not to be geologically significant. The remaining 23
analyses can be divided into two groups, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb* ratios.

                                                              2
Group I comprises seven analyses of five zircons (Table 1), which yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 2793 ± 8
Ma (MSWD = 1.3).

Group X comprises 16 analyses of 15 zircons (Table 1), which yield 207Pb*/206Pb* dates of 3031–2922 Ma. Fourteen
analyses of 13 zircons in Group X yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 2947 ± 5 Ma (MSWD = 1.2).
The date of 2793 ± 8 Ma for the seven analyses in Group I is interpreted as the magmatic crystallization age. The dates of
3031–2922 Ma for the 16 analyses in Group X are interpreted as the ages of inherited zircons. The date of 2947 ± 5 Ma for
14 analyses in Group X is interpreted as the age of an inherited component.

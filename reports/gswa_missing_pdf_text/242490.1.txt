                       242490.1: metamonzogranite, Wogara Bore
                (Big Bell Suite, Austin Downs Supersuite; Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                                SS Romano, MTD Wingate, Y LU, IOH Fielding and TJ Ivanic


Location and sampling
BELELE SG50-11, KALLI 2344                                                        Zone 50, MGA 502693E 7031231N
Warox Site TJIKAL190115                                                                Sampled on 2 September 2019
This sample was collected from bouldery outcrop on the north side of the Boolardy-Kalli Road on Kalli station, about 3.4
km west of 5 Mile Bore, 3.1 km southwest of Mudgeanoo Pool, and 2.8 km east of Wogara Bore.

Geological context
The unit sampled is a metamonzogranite (Fig. 1) of the 2732–2686 Ma Big Bell Suite of the Austin Downs Supersuite (Van
Kranendonk et al., 2013). The Big Bell Suite consists mainly of porphyritic and equigranular metagranodiorite to
metamonzogranite, which commonly exhibits a magmatic foliation (Zibra, 2021; ENS report Big Bell Suite, viewed 5
August 2023). A metamonzogranite sampled about 21 km to the west yielded an igneous crystallization age of 2701 ± 5
Ma (GSWA 242491, Romano et al., 2023). Several granitic samples collected about 37 km to the north yielded igneous
crystallization ages of 2717–2680 Ma (e.g. GSWA 105017, Nelson, 1996). A tonalite gneiss collected about 91 km to the
southeast yielded an igneous crystallization age of 2724 ± 2 Ma (GSWA 185923, Wingate et al., 2011).

Petrographic description
This sample is an inequigranular, fine to medium-grained metamonzogranite, consisting of about 35% plagioclase, 30% K-
feldspar, 25% quartz, 7% biotite, 2–3% myrmekite, <1% magnetite and accessory allanite, titanite, zircon and apatite.
Plagioclase is anhedral to subhedral, equant to slightly elongate, up to 2.5 mm long, and weakly saussuritized. K-feldspar is
anhedral, irregular, microperthitic, up to 2 mm in size, and exhibits microcline twinning. Some K-feldspar grains contain
abundant rounded quartz inclusions. Quartz is anhedral, irregular, strained, up to 1.5 mm in size, and interstitial to feldspar.
The quartz–feldspar aggregate exhibits a semi-polygonal texture. Green–brown biotite is anhedral to subhedral, up to 1.5
mm in size, and partially replaced by chlorite. Myrmekite forms lobes up to 0.3 mm diameter around K-feldspar margins.
Magnetite is anhedral to subhedral, equant, pitted and up to 0.5 mm in size. Allanite is euhedral, equant to slightly elongate,
and up to 0.45 mm long, and titanite is anhedral and up to 0.3 mm in size. Apatite is euhedral and up to 0.2 mm long.

Although this rock is foliated in outcrop, the thin section lacks a penetrative tectonic fabric but contains abundant evidence
of deformation and recovery at moderate metamorphic grade, in the form of microcline twinning, strain in quartz,
myrmekite and semi-polygonal texture.

Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are colourless to dark brown or opaque and subhedral to euhedral. The crystals are up to
400 µm long, and equant to elongate, with aspect ratios up to 6:1. In cathodoluminescence (CL) images, concentric zoning
is ubiquitous, inclusions of other minerals are abundant, and some crystals appear to contain older cores. A CL image of
representative zircons is shown in Figure 2.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 11–12 February 2022, using SHRIMP-B. Nine analyses of the M257 standard obtained during
the session indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 0.53% (1σ) and a 238U/206Pb* calibration
uncertainty of 0.26% (1σ). Calibration uncertainties are included in the errors of 238U/206Pb* ratios and dates listed in
Table 1 (provided in the Links section). Common-Pb corrections were applied to all analyses using contemporaneous
isotopic compositions determined according to the model of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
Fourteen analyses were obtained from 14 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1, and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig. 3).

Interpretation
The analyses are slightly discordant to moderately discordant (Fig. 3). One analysis is >5% discordant. The date obtained
from this analysis (Group D; Table 1) is unreliable and is considered not to be geologically significant. The remaining 13
analyses can be divided into two groups, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb* and Th/U ratios.

Group I comprises 11 analyses (Table 1), which yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 2693 ± 7 Ma (MSWD =
                                                              2
1.9). These analyses indicate mainly moderate to high Th/U ratios of 0.43 to 1.96 (median 1.16).

Group X comprises two analyses (Table 1), which yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 2724 ± 8 Ma (MSWD =
0.062). These analyses indicate low Th/U ratios of 0.33 and 0.49.
The date of 2693 ± 7 Ma for the 11 analyses in Group I is interpreted as the magmatic crystallization age of the
monzogranite. The date of 2724 ± 8 Ma for the two analyses in Group X is interpreted as the age of an inherited
component.

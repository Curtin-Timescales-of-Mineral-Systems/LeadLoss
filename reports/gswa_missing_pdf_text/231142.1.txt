      231142.1: hornblende–biotite metagranodiorite, Mount Dwyer
                (Big Bell Suite, Austin Downs Supersuite; Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                                SS Romano, Y Lu, IOH Fielding, MTD Wingate and TJ Ivanic


Location and sampling
YOUANMI SH50-04, EVERETT CREEK 2841                                                 Zone 50, MGA 746202E 6881296N
Warox Site TJISST180046                                                                       Sampled on 29 May 2018
This sample was collected from the 327.2 – 327.7 m depth interval of diamond drillcore TDD036, drilled in 2008 by Troy
Resources NL at their Sandstone gold project. The drillhole is located about 4.7 km east of Marley Well, 1.7 km south-
southeast of Mount Dwyer, and 1.0 km north of the Menzies – Sandstone Road.

Geological context
The unit sampled is a metagranodiorite of the Big Bell Suite (Zibra 2021). The Big Bell Suite is part of the Austin Downs
Supersuite and is exposed over a wide area in the northern Youanmi Terrane in the western Yilgarn Craton. It consists
mainly of K-feldspar-porphyritic and equigranular monzogranite and were emplaced between c. 2732 and 2686 Ma. The
geochemistry of the Big Bell Suite shows a dominant tonalite–trondhjemite–granodiorite (TTG) affinity. The sampled
granodiorite has intruded the boundary between the Meekatharra Formation that has been deposited between 2800-2760
Ma and unassigned greenstones of the Sandstone greenstone belt. A metatonalite 13.5 km to the west yielded a maximum
crystallization age of 2740 ± 4 Ma (GSWA 229355, Wingate et al. 2020). Two foliated biotite granodiorites sampled about
13.6 and 12.3 km to the north yielded igneous crystallization age of 2686 ± 5 Ma (GSWA 165365, Nelson, 2004) and 2691
± 3 Ma (GSWA 169070, Nelson, 2002), respectively.

Petrographic description
The sample is a medium-grained, equigranular hornblende–biotite metagranodiorite, consisting of about 40–45%
plagioclase, 30–35% quartz, 10% K-feldspar, 10% biotite, 3–4% hornblende, 1% magnetite and accessory apatite and
zircon. Plagioclase has mostly been saussuritized, whereas biotite has been partially replaced by chlorite and hornblende by
actinolite. Plagioclase is tabular, subhedral to anhedral, and up to 2.5 mm in size. Rare relict plagioclase grains show
andesine composition (around An38). Quartz is anhedral, up to 2 mm in size with interdigitating grain boundaries and
exhibits undulose extinction. K-feldspar is perthitic, occurring as anhedral grains up to 2 mm in size. Brown biotite forms
                                                              1
stubby subhedral grains up to 1.5 mm in size. Subhedral and euhedral hornblende grains up to 1.7 mm in size are pale to
olive green. Magnetite occurs as equant, subhedral and rarely euhedral grains that range from <0.1 to 0.3 mm. Apatite
forms prismatic euhedral grains to 0.15 mm in length. The granodiorite lacks a tectonic foliation but has undergone solid-
state deformation at relatively low grade.

Zircon morphology
Zircons from this sample are colourless to dark brown, and subhedral to euhedral. The crystals are up to 230 µm long, and
mainly elongate, with aspect ratios up to 7:1. In cathodoluminescence (CL) images, concentric zoning is ubiquitous, and a
few crystals appear to contain older cores. A CL image of representative zircons is shown in Figure 2.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 21–22 February 2019, using SHRIMP-B. Eleven analyses of the M257 standard obtained
during the session indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 0.77% (1σ) and a 238U/206Pb*
calibration uncertainty of 0.28% (1σ). Calibration uncertainties are included in the errors of 238U/206Pb* ratios and dates
listed in Table 1 (provided in the Links section). Common-Pb corrections were applied to all analyses using
contemporaneous isotopic compositions determined according to the model of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
Twenty-three analyses were obtained from 23 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1 and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig.
3).

Interpretation
The analyses are slightly discordant to moderately discordant (Fig. 3). Two analyses are >5% discordant. The dates
obtained from these two analyses (Group D; Table 1) are unreliable and are considered not to be geologically significant.
The remaining 21 analyses form a single group, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb* ratios.

Group I comprises 21 analyses of 21 zircons (Table 1), which yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 2701 ± 3 Ma
(MSWD = 0.90).
The date of 2701 ± 3 Ma for the 21 analyses in Group I is interpreted as the igneous crystallization age of the granodiorite.

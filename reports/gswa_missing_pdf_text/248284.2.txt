                    248284.2: metamonzogranite, Merino prospect
                          (Yilgarn Craton granites; Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                                      IOH Fielding, Y Lu, MTD Wingate and TJ Ivanic


Location and sampling
CORRIGIN SI50-03, NAREMBEEN 2533                                                    Zone 50, MGA 636856E 6440455N
Warox Site TJISWT000026                                                                 Sampled on 22 October 2020
This sample was collected from the 72.4 – 74.0 m depth interval of diamond drillcore THDD025, drilled in 2018 by
Explaurum Operations Pty Ltd at the Merino prospect of their Tampia gold project (Franey, 2019). The drillhole is located
about 5.6 km west of Rodger Road, 2.8 km southwest of Mount Arrowsmith, and 2.2 km north of Tampia Hill.

Geological context
The unit sampled is a metamonzogranite (Fig. 1) within the western Youanmi Terrane of the Yilgarn Craton (Quentin de
Gromard et al., 2021). Monazites from this sample yielded an age of metamorphism of 2617 ± 8 Ma (Fielding et al, 2022).
A metagranodiorite sampled about 30 km to the north yielded an igneous crystallization age of 2657 ± 5 Ma (GSWA
250432, Wingate et al., 2021). A high-Ca monzogranite sampled about 31 km to the east-northeast yielded U–Pb zircon
dates of c. 2680–2630 Ma (Qui and McNaughton, 1999).

Petrographic description
The sample is an equigranular, medium-grained, cordierite–orthopyroxene–garnet-bearing metamonzogranite (Fig. 1),
consisting of about 30–35% K-feldspar, 30% quartz, 25% plagioclase, 3% myrmekite, 3% biotite, 2% garnet, 1%
orthopyroxene, 1% magnetite, <1% cordierite and accessory muscovite, sulfide minerals and zircon. K-feldspar is anhedral
to subhedral, up to 4.5 mm long. Larger grains are microperthitic, some exhibit microcline twinning, and many contain
round inclusions of quartz and plagioclase. Quartz is anhedral, irregular, up to 6 mm in size, weakly strained, and exhibits
smooth, curved boundaries with adjacent feldspar grains resulting in a semipolygonal texture. Plagioclase (andesine, An32)
is anhedral to subhedral, equant to slightly tabular, and up to 4 mm in size. Curved lobes of myrmekite occur at the margins
of some K-feldspar grains. Biotite forms anhedral skeletal laths up to 1.5 mm long, intergrown with quartz, and smaller
bladed laths, some of which contain randomly oriented acicular rutile. Garnet is anhedral, embayed, fractured and up to 4.5

mm in size. Orthopyroxene (hypersthene) is subhedral, equant, up to 1 mm in size and associated with biotite. Cordierite is
anhedral and >2 mm in size. Magnetite is anhedral to subhedral, equant, embayed, and up to 0.8 mm in diameter. Muscovite
forms anhedral and subhedral laths up to 0.5 mm long and is partly replaced by carbonate. The sample lacks a tectonic
foliation, although its semipolygonal texture, abundance of microcline and myrmekite, indicate it has been deformed and
partly recovered at moderate metamorphic conditions (at least amphibolite facies, based on the presence of cordierite). The
sample retains its igneous texture, and the garnet and orthopyroxene may be primary rather than metamorphic.

Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are colourless to dark brown and mainly subhedral, and slightly rounded. The crystals are
up to 450 µm long, and equant to slightly elongate, with aspect ratios up to 4:1. In cathodoluminescence (CL) images,
most zircons exhibit concentric zoning, and some appear to contain older cores. A CL image of representative zircons is
shown in Figure 2.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 3–4 June 2021, using SHRIMP-B. Twelve analyses of the M257 standard were obtained
during the session, of which 10 analyses indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 0.50% (1σ) and a
238U/206Pb* calibration uncertainty of 0.15% (1σ). Isotopic mass fractionation of 207Pb*/206Pb* ratios during the session
was corrected by reference to the OGC1 standard; measured ratios were increased by 0.24%. Calibration uncertainties are
included in the errors of 238U/206Pb* ratios and dates listed in Table 1 (provided in the Links section). Common-Pb
corrections were applied to all analyses using contemporaneous isotopic compositions determined according to the model
of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
Nineteen analyses were obtained from 18 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1 and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig. 3).

Interpretation
The analyses are concordant to strongly discordant (Fig. 3). Four analyses are >5% discordant. The dates obtained from
these four analyses (Group D; Table 1) are unreliable and considered not to be geologically significant. The remaining 15
analyses form a single group, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb* ratios.

Group I comprises 15 analyses of 15 zircons (Table 1), which yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 2639 ± 7 Ma
(MSWD = 2.2).
The date of 2639 ± 7 Ma for the 15 analyses in Group I is interpreted as the igneous crystallization age of the
metamonzogranite.

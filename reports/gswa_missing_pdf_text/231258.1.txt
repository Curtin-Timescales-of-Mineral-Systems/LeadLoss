                      231258.1: metagranodiorite, Stockyard Well
                              (Tuckanarra Suite; Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                               SS Romano, Y Lu, IOH Fielding, MTD Wingate and TJ Ivanic


Location and sampling
MURGOO SG50-14, MURGOO 2143                                                        Zone 50, MGA 402430E 6984520N
Warox Site TJIMUR190058                                                               Sampled on 4 September 2019
This sample was collected from an outcrop (Fig. 1) about 7.9 km north-northeast of Mount Hope, 6.6 km west-southwest
of Woolbung Well, and 4.6 km east of Stockyard Well.

Geological context
The unit sampled is a metagranodiorite of the Tuckanarra Suite of the Yilgarn Craton (Van Kranendonk et al., 2013),
which consists of strongly foliated and locally magmatically layered rocks ranging in composition from granodiorite to
monzogranite that were emplaced between c. 2697 and 2657 Ma (Zibra, 2019). The maximum age of the Tuckanarra Suite
is based on the 2697 ± 9 Ma crystallization age of a fine-grained metatonalite dated by Wang (1998). The minimum age of
the suite is defined by the crystallization age of 2657 ± 9 Ma obtained from the youngest phase of the Cundimurra
Monzogranite. Components of this suite lie within, and adjacent to, large-scale shear zones (Zibra, 2019).

Petrographic description
The sample is a weakly foliated, inequigranular, medium-grained metagranodiorite, consisting of about 40–45%
plagioclase, 20–25% quartz, 15% K-feldspar, 10% biotite, 6–7% epidote, 3% titanite, 2% hematite and accessory allanite,
apatite and zircon. Plagioclase mainly occurs as small (<1 – 2.5 mm), anhedral, and equant grains in an aggregate with
quartz and K-feldspar with some tabular plagioclase up to 7 mm in length being partly substituted by microcline and quartz.
Most plagioclase has been saussuritized. Recrystallised anhedral quartz forms irregular patches <2 mm in size. K-feldspar is
microcline and occurs as either tabular grains up to 5.5 mm in size, or more commonly as anhedral equant grains <0.5 mm
in diameter in semi-polygonal aggregates with quartz and plagioclase. Larger green-brown biotite (<1.5 mm) is weakly
aligned and associated with lense-shaped aggregates of much smaller recrystallized biotite grains. Subhedral and anhedral

epidote is up to 1 mm in size. Titanite forms anhedral grains to almost 2 mm in size. Magnetite and minor hematite occur as
pitted, mainly anhedral, equant grains to 0.8 mm in size. Relict anhedral (0.25 – 1 mm) allanite has been replaced by yellow
clay and iron oxides. Apatite occurs as mostly slender euhedral laths to 0.25 mm.

Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are pale brown to dark brown and subhedral to euhedral. The crystals are up to 400 µm
long, and stumpy to elongate, with aspect ratios up to 9:1. In cathodoluminescence (CL) images, concentric zoning is
ubiquitous, and the cores of some crystals exhibit embayments consistent with magmatic resorption. Many zircons contain
inclusions and feature high U-rims, which are typically metamict. A CL image of representative zircons is shown in Figure
2.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 14–15 October 2021, using SHRIMP-B. Thirteen analyses of the M257 standard were
obtained during the session, of which 12 analyses indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 0.50%
(1σ) and a 238U/206Pb* calibration uncertainty of 0.31% (1σ). Calibration uncertainties are included in the errors of
238U/206Pb* ratios and dates listed in Table 1 (provided in the Links section). Common-Pb corrections were applied to all
analyses using contemporaneous isotopic compositions determined according to the model of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
Sixteen analyses were obtained from 12 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1 and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig. 3).

Interpretation
The analyses are concordant to moderately discordant (Fig. 3). Four analyses are >5% discordant. The dates obtained from
these four analyses (Group D; Table 1) are unreliable and considered not to be geologically significant. The remaining 12
analyses form a single group, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb* ratios.

Group I comprises 12 analyses of nine zircons (Table 1), which yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 2674 ± 4 Ma
(MSWD = 0.43).
The date of 2674 ± 4 Ma for the 12 analyses in Group I is interpreted as the igneous crystallization age of the granodiorite.

                   242439.1: granodiorite gneiss, Mt Marvel quarry
                          (Yilgarn Craton granites; Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                                SS Romano, Y Lu, IOH Fielding, MTD Wingate and TJ Ivanic


Location and sampling
PERENJORI SH50-06, PERENJORI 2139                                                 Zone 50, MGA 411691E 6780029N
Warox Site TJIROT190059                                                                     Sampled on 15 May 2019
This sample was collected from the southern side of the Mount Marvel quarry, about 10.6 km east of Pintharuka, 3.6 km
northwest of Pinjarra Hill, and 1.7 km west of the Morawa Yalgoo Road.

Geological context
The unit sampled is a granodiorite gneiss of Yilgarn Craton granites (Fig. 1). A metamonzogranite of the Bunnawarra
Suite, Thundelarra Supersuite, sampled about 32 km to the east, yielded an igneous crystallization age of 2946 ± 4 Ma
(GSWA 219311, Wingate et al. 2020). Although the 3010–2930 Ma Thundelarra Supersuite was previously considered the
oldest basement unit in the Youanmi Terrane (Ivanic et al., 2022), the igneous crystallization age of this sample discussed
below suggests existence of even older basement up to at least c. 3275 Ma in the Youanmi Terrane.

Petrographic description
The sample is a fine- to medium-grained, equigranular biotite granodiorite gneiss, consisting of about 55% plagioclase,
25% quartz, 15% K-feldspar, 5% biotite and accessory magnetite, apatite and zircon. Plagioclase occurs as subhedral,
mainly equant to slightly tabular grains to 2.5 mm in size with most grains altered by sericite and minor muscovite. Quartz
occurs as anhedral grains up to 1 mm in size, forming irregular aggregates between larger feldspar grains, or as
recrystallized polygonal grains to 0.1 mm in diameter throughout the sample. K-feldspar is perthitic, occurs as anhedral
grains to 2 mm in diameter, and is often fringed by recrystallized polygonal grains of mainly K-feldspar. Some relict K-
feldspar contains inclusions of plagioclase and quartz. Biotite is brown and occurs as subhedral laths to 0.5 mm in length
within small aggregates of either decussate or well-aligned grains, mostly around the margins of feldspar. Biotite is also a
minor component of the recrystallized aggregates. Magnetite occurs as disseminated, anhedral and subhedral grains within
small aggregates of biotite. Apatite occurs as euhedral grains to 0.1 mm in size. Partial recovery involving grain boundary
adjustment in quartz and minor fine-grained recrystallization indicate a moderate metamorphic overprint.

Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are colourless to dark brown or opaque and subhedral to euhedral. The crystals are up to
250 µm long, and equant to elongate, with aspect ratios up to 5:1. In cathodoluminescence (CL) images, concentric zoning
is ubiquitous, and some crystals appear to contain older or metamict cores. A CL image of representative zircons is shown
in Figure 2.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 10–11 February 2022, using SHRIMP-B. Fourteen analyses of the M257 standard were
obtained during the session, of which 12 analyses indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 0.50%
(1σ) and a 238U/206Pb* calibration uncertainty of 0.14% (1σ). Calibration uncertainties are included in the errors of
238U/206Pb* ratios and dates listed in Table 1 (provided in the Links section). Common-Pb corrections were applied to all
analyses using contemporaneous isotopic compositions determined according to the model of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
Eighteen analyses were obtained from 18 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1 and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig. 3).

Interpretation
The analyses are concordant to moderately discordant (Fig. 2). One analysis is >8% discordant. The date obtained from this
analysis (Group D, Table 1) is unreliable and considered not to be geologically significant. The remaining 17 analyses can
be divided into four groups, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb* ratios.

Group I comprises 11 analyses (Table 1), which yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 3275 ± 5 Ma (MSWD =
2.1).

Group X comprises one analysis of a zircon core (Table 1), which yields a 207Pb*/206Pb* date of 3506 ± 4 Ma (1σ).

                                                             2
Group P comprises three analyses (Table 1), which yield 207Pb*/206Pb* dates of 3260–3250 Ma.

Group Z comprises two analyses (Table 1), which yield 207Pb*/206Pb* dates of c. 1492 and 1491 Ma.
The date of 3275 ± 5 Ma for the 11 analyses in Group I is interpreted as the igneous crystallization age of the granodiorite
protolith.
The date of 3506 ± 4 Ma for the single analysis in Group X is interpreted as the age of an inherited component. The dates
of 3260–3250 Ma for the three analyses in Group P are interpreted to reflect minor ancient loss of radiogenic Pb. The dates
of c. 1492 and 1491 Ma for the two analyses in Group Z are interpreted as the ages of contaminants introduced during
sample processing.

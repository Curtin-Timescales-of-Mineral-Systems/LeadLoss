                       214139.1: monzogranite gneiss, Joker Well
    (Goonetarra Granodiorite, Rothsay Suite, Annean Supersuite; Youanmi Terrane, Yilgarn Craton)
                                        IOH Fielding, Y Lu, MTD Wingate and I Zibra


Location and sampling
YALGOO SH50-02, YALGOO 2241                                                       Zone 50, MGA 474451E 6850870N
Warox Site AETYLG000113                                                                    Sampled on 24 May 2014
This sample was collected from a boulder-style outcrop on Bunnawarra Station, about 22.5 km northeast of Bunnawarra
Homestead, 15.2 km northwest of Badja Homestead, and 4.8 km southwest of Joker Well.

Geological context
The unit sampled is the Goonetarra Granodiorite of the Rothsay Suite, Annean Supersuite (Van Kranendonk et al., 2013;
Zibra, 2020; ENS Report Goonetarra Granodiorite, viewed 28 April 2023). The 2763–2749 Ma Goonetarra Granodiorite
consists mainly of metatonalite, metagranodiorite and metamonzogranite, which are typically foliated and lineated (Zibra,
2020: ENS Goonetarra Granodiorite, viewed 28 April 2023). In the Yalgoo dome, the Goonetarra Granodiorite is intruded
into the Kynea Tonalite and into the greenstone succession (Zibra et al., 2018; Clos et al., 2018). Near the centre of the
Yalgoo dome, the Goonetarra Granodiorite has intruded by monzogranite of the 2650–2600 Ma Walganna Suite of the
Bald Rock Supersuite (Zibra, 2020: ENS Report Goonetarra Granodiorite, viewed 28 April 2023). A granodiorite gneiss
and monzogranite gneiss of the Goonterra Granodiorite sampled about 90 m to the southwest and 1.5 km to the east-
southeast yielded crystallization ages of c. 2763 Ma (GSWA 214138, Fielding et al., 2023b; GSWA 214101, Lu et al.,
2017).

Petrographic description
The sample is a massive to weakly foliated, medium-grained monzogranite gneiss, consisting of about 35–40% plagioclase,
30–35% K-feldspar, 25% quartz, 5% biotite, 1–2% opaque minerals, 1% myrmekite, and accessory apatite, allanite and
zircon. Plagioclase is anhedral, equant to tabular, up to 3.5 mm in length and poorly twinned. Some grains exhibit a distinct
core marked by very fine sericite. K-feldspar occurs as anhedral, equant to slightly elongate grains that are up to 4 mm
long. Many have very fine microperthitic texture and microcline twinning, and some of the larger grains contain small
inclusions of plagioclase, quartz and biotite. K-feldspar porphyroclasts are wrapped by elongate trails of biotite and quartz
ribbons, defining the metamorphic foliation. Strain-induced myrmekites (<0.5 mm) commonly occur along K-feldspar
margins parallel to the foliation. Quartz is weakly to moderately strained and occurs as irregular aggregates intergrown with
plagioclase and K-feldspar. Finer grained aggregates of quartz and microcline have a polygonal texture. Biotite is partially
replaced by chlorite and epidote and occurs as subhedral to anhedral laths, up to 1.5 mm long. Subhedral opaque grains up
to 0.7 mm in diameter are probable magnetite. The accessory minerals include subhedral, equant to tabular apatite up to
0.15 mm, metamict allanite up to 0.25 mm in diameter and dark brown zircon that are located within biotite.
Microstructures indicate that the main fabric developed under mid- to low-amphibolite facies conditions.

Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are colourless to dark brown, and subhedral to euhedral. The crystals are up to 500 µm
long, and elongate, with aspect ratios up to 5:1. In cathodoluminescence (CL) images, concentric zoning is ubiquitous.
Many crystals contain high uranium, metamict zones. A CL image of representative zircons is shown in Figure 1.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 18–19 May 2015, using SHRIMP-B. Ten analyses of the BR266 standard were obtained
during the session. Significant drift of standard 238U/206Pb* dates during the session was addressed by fitting a LOWESS
curve (Cleveland, 1979) with a smoothing window of six analyses, implemented using the program Isoplot 2.50 (Ludwig,
2009; Fielding et al., 2023a). During the session, ten standard analyses indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility)
uncertainty of 1.10% (1σ). Calibration uncertainties are included in the errors of 238U/206Pb* ratios and dates listed in
Table 1 (provided in the Links section). Common-Pb corrections were applied to all analyses using contemporaneous
isotopic compositions determined according to the model of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
Twelve analyses were obtained from 12 zircons. Results are listed in Table 1 and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig. 2).

Interpretation
The analyses are concordant to moderately discordant (Fig. 2), and form two groups, based on their 207Pb*/206Pb* ratios.

Group I comprises ten analyses of ten zircons (Table 1), which yield a weighted mean 207Pb*/206Pb** date of 2761 ± 4 Ma
(MSWD = 0.57).

Group P comprises two analyses of two zircons (Table 1), which yield 207Pb*/206Pb* dates of 2741 ± 6 Ma and 2747 ± 4
Ma (1σ).
The date of 2761 ± 4 Ma for the ten analyses in Group I is interpreted as the magmatic crystallization age of the
monzogranite protolith.
The dates of 2741 and 2747 Ma for the two analyses in Group P are interpreted to reflect minor ancient loss of radiogenic
Pb.

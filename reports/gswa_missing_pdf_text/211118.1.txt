                   211118.1: lamprophyre dyke, Deflector prospect
                                                     (Yilgarn Craton)
                                SS Romano, MTD Wingate, Y LU, IOH Fielding and TJ Ivanic


Location and sampling
YALGOO SH50-02, MELLENBYE 2140                                                     Zone 50, MGA 439143E 6828066N
Warox Site TJIBYE000006                                                                       Sampled on 20 May 2019
This sample was collected from the 174.0 – 174.5 m depth interval of diamond drillcore BDD018, drilled in 2006 by
Batavia Mining Ltd at their Deflector gold–copper prospect (Mattinson, 2007). The drillhole is located about 8.0 km west-
northwest of Buddadoo Well, 7.5 km east-northeast of Yallabyne Well, and 0.3 km south of the Deflector West open pit.

Geological context
The unit sampled is a metamorphosed lamprophyre dyke within the Gullewa greenstone belt of the Yilgarn Craton. The
dyke is intruded into metamorphosed volcanogenic and sedimentary rocks of the 2800–2735 Ma Polelle Group (Mattinson,
2007; Van Kranendonk et al., 2013). A minette dyke with sharp margins was identified at a depth of 109.9 m in this
drillcore (sample 42266, Appendix 1, Bishop, 2007). An altered polymictic breccia of the Mougooderra Formation (Zibra,
2022: ENS report Mougooderra Formation, viewed 2 August 2023), sampled in drillcore BDRCD075 about 0.2 km to the
northwest, yielded 3087–2625 Ma detrital zircons and a conservative maximum depositional age of 2672 ± 10 Ma (GSWA
211120, Wingate et al., 2021).

Petrographic description
The sample is a fine to medium-grained lamprophyre, consisting of about 60% feldspar, 18% biotite, 12% actinolite, 5%
quartz, 4% magnetite, 1% titanite, trace hornblende and accessory apatite. Secondary minerals include quartz, carbonate
and trace pyrite and chalcopyrite. Feldspar is anhedral to tabular, up to 0.7 mm long, and forms aggregates of randomly
oriented laths. Based on twinning, K-feldspar is more abundant than plagioclase, which is albitized, and all feldspars are
overprinted by fine-grained biotite, amphibole and magnetite. A trace amount of granophyre is present within the feldspar
aggregates. Brown biotite is anhedral to euhedral and forms randomly oriented grains up to 2 mm long, as well as fine-
grained aggregates around larger biotite laths, in feldspar-rich domains, and along fractures. Pale green actinolite occurs as
ragged relicts, up to 1 mm in size, of original subhedral laths, and as dispersed grains up to 0.1 mm in size. Quartz is
                                                               1
interstitial, anhedral, irregular, up to 0.25 mm in size, and appears to be mostly secondary. Magnetite forms anhedral to
subhedral, equant grains up to 0.3 mm in diameter, and also disseminated micron-scale grains. Irregular, granular masses of
titanite are up to 0.7 mm in size and associated with actinolite. Tabular subhedral to euhedral apatite is up to 0.7 mm long.
Hornblende is subhedral and up to 0.5 mm. Pyrite forms cubic grains up to 0.5 mm across and anhedral chalcopyrite is
associated with actinolite. The sample lacks obvious features of deformation, although fine-grained biotite and actinolite are
interpreted to be products of contact metamorphism at biotite to hornblende hornfels grade.

Zircon morphology
Zircons isolated from this sample are colourless and anhedral to subhedral. The crystals are up to 120 µm long, and equant,
with aspect ratios up to 2:1. In cathodoluminescence (CL) images all zircons exhibit concentric zoning. A CL image of all
zircons is shown in Figure 2.

Analytical details
This sample was analysed on 21–22 October 2021, using SHRIMP-B. Fifteen analyses of the M257 standard were obtained
during the session, of which 11 analyses indicated an external spot-to-spot (reproducibility) uncertainty of 0.80% (1σ) and a
238U/206Pb* calibration uncertainty of 0.32% (1σ). Calibration uncertainties are included in the errors of 238U/206Pb*
ratios and dates listed in Table 1 (provided in the Links section). Common-Pb corrections were applied to all analyses using
contemporaneous isotopic compositions determined according to the model of Stacey and Kramers (1975).

Results
Five analyses were obtained from five zircons. Results are listed in Table 1, and shown in a concordia diagram (Fig. 3).

Interpretation
The analyses are concordant to moderately discordant (Fig. 3). One analysis is >5% discordant. The date obtained from this
analysis (Table 1, Group D) is unreliable, and considered not to be geologically significant. The remaining four analyses
form a single dispersed group.

Group X comprises four analyses (Table 1), which yield 207Pb*/206Pb* dates of 2871–2624 Ma.
The dates of 2871–2624 Ma for the four analyses in Group X are interpreted as the ages of inherited zircons. The date of
2624 ± 13 Ma (1σ) for the youngest zircon in Group X is tentatively interpreted as a maximum age for intrusion and
igneous crystallization of the lamprophyre dyke.
